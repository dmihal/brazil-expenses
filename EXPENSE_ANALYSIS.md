### Pro-Ration Methodology

Instead of splitting the total equally (which would be unfair to those who ordered cheaper meals), we used a **proportional distribution** based on each person's meal base price.

#### Step 1: Calculate each person's proportion of the meal subtotal
- **Total meal subtotal**: R$ 425.70
- Each person's proportion = `(Their Base Price) √∑ 425.70`

#### Step 2: Apply proportion to adjustments
- **Net adjustment** = (Coupon) + (Delivery) + (Service) = ‚àí10.00 + 17.99 + 1.99 = **+9.98**
- Each person pays their proportion of the +R$ 9.98

#### Step 3: Final per-person cost
- **Final share** = Base Price + (Proportion √ó 9.98)

### Why This Methodology?

**‚ùå Not split equally**: If we split R$ 435.68 equally among 6 people = R$ 72.61 each
- **Problem**: Nick & Bruna ordered cheaper meals but would pay the same as Joseph & Antonio who ordered premium meals with add-ons
- **Unfair**: Penalizes people with cheaper orders

**‚úÖ Proportional pro-ration**: Each person pays their share of fees based on what they ordered
- **Benefit**: People who ordered expensive meals pay more fees; people who ordered cheap meals pay fewer fees
- **Fair**: Aligns costs with consumption
- **Standard practice**: This is how restaurants/platforms typically handle split bills

**Result**: The R$ 9.98 adjustment was distributed fairly across all 6 people.
